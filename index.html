Why this workshop

Fairness in educational AI has mostly been defined by the people who build it.

A demographic disparity like the one above is one form of algorithmic unfairness, and it can take many shapes across the automated decisions universities make about students. How far that unfairness erodes students' trust is still poorly understood — and so far, deciding what counts as fair has largely fallen to system developers, with limited input from educators, institutions, or the students affected.

We take the view that fairness in education warrants broader deliberation than developers alone can provide. This workshop is where that deliberation happens.

What we'll examine

Across the decisions that raise the most serious concerns, we'll ask what fair treatment requires.

Three questions sit at the centre of every case we look at.

Inputs

What may a decision use?

Which pieces of student information an automated decision should be allowed to draw on in the first place.

Transparency

How openly is it explained?

How clearly the decision, and the reasons behind it, are made visible to the student it affects.

Treatment

Identical, or adjusted?

Whether every student should be treated the same way, or allowances made for personal circumstances.

What it builds

From the room to a reusable instrument.

The ideas contributed at the workshop feed a survey, the survey is checked by the people who shaped it, and the result becomes a framework others can use.

01

Deliberate the cases

Participants work through the automated decisions that raise the sharpest fairness concerns and weigh what fair treatment should require.

02

Draft the survey instrument

Those ideas become a draft instrument that measures how fair students themselves consider a decision to be — and how that judgement shapes their trust.

03

Return it for review async · ~30–45 min

The draft goes back to participants in a short follow-up to confirm it reads clearly and realistically before it reaches any student.

04

Administer to students

The refined instrument is put to students, whose judgements of fairness and trust are what the study is ultimately about.

05

Grow into a framework

In time the instrument forms the basis of a broader framework for fairness and trust that can be reused well beyond this study.
